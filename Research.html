Research interests

    Primary research interests

    My research aims to advance the application of deep learning techniques to solve forward and inverse problems governed by partial differential equations (PDEs). By integrating traditional mathematical methods with modern deep-learning approaches, I aim to improve the efficiency and accuracy of solutions for real-world applications, including image restoration, medical imaging, and related fields.

    • Deep learning and Bayesian inverse problems

      In Bayesian inverse problems, using the Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is prohibitively time-consuming. Recently, we propose an MCMC-Net, a simple yet efficient way to accelerate MCMC via deep neural networks (DNNs). The key idea of our approach is to substitute the likelihood function of the MCMC method with a convolution neural operator. We extensively evaluate the accuracy and speedup of our method on three different PDE-based inverse problems where likelihood computations are computationally expensive, namely electrical impedance tomography, diffuse optical tomography, and quantitative photoacoustic tomography [Arxiv].

      The advantage of using deep learning for operator approximation lies in its ability to generalize from training data to unseen scenarios, enabling rapid predictions without requiring extensive re-computation. This is particularly beneficial in real-time applications and scenarios requiring repeated evaluations of operators, such as in MCMC-based algorithms for posterior exploration.

    • Development of new image restoration models based on partial differential equations

      My doctoral study focused on the development and analysis of PDE-based models for image restoration. Specifically, I proposed several hyperbolic-parabolic type PDEs to address image denoising problems when images are degraded by additive Gaussian and multiplicative speckle noise. I investigated the theoretical properties of each proposed model, including the existence, uniqueness, and boundedness of weak solutions. To demonstrate the effectiveness of these models, I conducted numerical experiments on various types of gray-scale and color images with artificial noise. Additionally, I applied the proposed models to real-world images, such as Synthetic Aperture Radar and Ultrasound images [Ph.D. thesis].

    Other research interests

    • Image processing techniques based on partial differential equations

      During my Ph.D. research, I primarily focused on the development and analysis of Partial Differential Equation (PDE)-based models and their applications to image restoration. Additionally, I am interested in other image processing problems, such as inpainting, segmentation, color image processing, and related extensions. I have also collaborated with Mr. Ankit Kumar and Dr. Subit K. Jain on image segmentation [Journal].

    • Enhanced thermal imaging method

      I am collaborating on a thermal imaging project in the Department of Physics and Optical Science at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. This project focuses on investigating the potential of enhanced thermal imaging (ETI) as a non-invasive technique to quantify microvascular perfusion. Through MCmatlab simulations, we are modeling heat diffusion and photon deposition in the vascularized murine skin tissue to elucidate how thermal signals correlate with capillary density and blood flow. Our goal is to establish ETI as a cost-effective and reliable method for intraoperative guidance and the monitoring of tissue healing processes. While existing literature often relies on overly simplistic models, our current project emphasizes the development of more accurate, realistic models that better represent real-life scenarios, further advancing the potential applications of ETI.

    • Developing and exploring non-PDE frameworks for image processing

      In addition to the PDE-based approach, I explored the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM), a robust stochastic optimization technique for color image restoration. MEM is primarily used to address blurring effects and additive noise but has potential for extension to tackle multiplicative noise removal, particularly in medical and Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) imaging. Furthermore, I am interested in applying Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling techniques to image restoration problems, with an emphasis on integrating deep learning methods to enhance performance.
