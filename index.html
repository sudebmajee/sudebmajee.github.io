About Me

I am a postdoctoral fellow in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics, University of North Carolina at Charlotte. I received my Ph.D. from the Indian Institute of Technology Mandi, Himachal Pradesh, India, in January 2021. My research aims to advance the application of deep learning techniques to solve forward and inverse problems governed by partial differential equations (PDEs). By integrating traditional mathematical methods with modern deep learning approaches, I aim to improve the efficiency and accuracy of solutions for real-world applications, including image restoration, medical imaging, and related fields. Recently, my work investigates the integration of deep neural networks (DNNs) into Bayesian inverse problems, with an emphasis on using convolution neural operator to accelerate Markov Chain Monte Carlo method (MCMC). Also, I work on developing PDE-based models for image denoising and segmentation problems.
